Frequently Asked Questions

What is AEEB?

The Amicale des Elèves et Etudiants Babouantou (A.E.E.B) is a non-political and non-profit association created in 1964, under the name of Rassemblement des Elèves Babouantou (R.E.B); then Regroupement des Elèves et Etudiants Babouantou (R.E.E.B) and finally (AEEB.) It advocates the equality of all without any distinction. It wants to be a school of life, a circle of reflection and education. It intends to cultivate and promote the spirit of solidarity and mutual respect between its members. It intends to cultivate and promote the spirit of solidarity and mutual respect among its members, and to promote the culture of the Babouantou Group. It wants to be a nursery for the adult associations of tomorrow.

What are the objectives of AEEB?

To bring together the school and student youth of the Babouantou Group in order to to establish, maintain, preserve and consolidate the bonds of friendship and fraternity; To create and consolidate among its members the spirit of solidarity, understanding and mutual aid. To promote the development of the Babouantou people by the moralization of the behavior of this one to the intellectual works and of human investments. To promote the tradition and culture of the Babouantou Group; To maintain dialogue and harmony between its members, relatives and other associations. To encourage the instruction, the education and a good orientation and insertion of the Babouantou youth. To contribute to the development of the Babouantou Group by initiating and carrying out academic, social and cultural activities, To create and coordinate the action of the student associations of young people in the villages and the AEEB branches.

What are the activities carried on by AEEB?

In order to achieve these goals, the AEEB may organize the following activities: Summer camps organized once every 02 years. Capacity building courses (COREC) for school children, Periodic meetings in any locality where Babouantou nationals reside. Cultural weeks, debates, lectures on ethical issues, tradition, social facts and current affairs. Sports meetings with other associations. Socio-educational activities, excursions

What is the AEEB's motto?

SOLIDARITY - PROGRESS - SUCCESS

Who can join the AEEB?

Is a member by right of the Amicale des Elèves et Etudiants Babouantou, any Pupil, Student Babouantou regularly registered in a school or university. A sympathizing member is any young person not attending school who commits to respect the present General Statute, to submit to the Internal Regulations and to have paid his membership card.

What are the AEEB branches?

Babouantou, Yaounde, Douala, Bafang. Dschang, Bandjoun, Bafoussam (Cameroon).

How many years does an AEEB term last?

AEEB term last for 2years
